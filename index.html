Day 03 · Wednesday, March 25

Mauna Kea Summit
+ Manta Rays

Rain replaces mud hikes with a Waimea food crawl. The Jeep Wrangler unlocks the summit at 13,796 ft. Manta ray night snorkel closes out the evening.

Rain plan
White Road + Mud Lane removed — impassable when wet. Summit + mantas replace them.
Jeep Wrangler unlocks the Mauna Kea summit. Rangers at the VIS exit verify 4LO before allowing you past. Practice engaging it before you leave Kona on Day 1. Confirm in writing with your rental company that summit access is permitted — most major rental partners prohibit it contractually even if the vehicle is capable.
7:00 AM
Work
Work block + Mauna Kea weather check
Weather hotline: (808) 935-6268 — automated, updated daily. Summit webcams: mkwc.ifa.hawaii.edu. Summit may be icy or closed in March. If closed, VIS stargazing at 9,200 ft is still extraordinary.
9:00 AM
My additionBreakfast
Waimea Farmers Market at Keck Observatory HQ
Every Saturday. Local vendors, malasadas, fresh tropical produce, live music. Free entry. Waimea is directly on the route to Saddle Road. Grab a walk-around breakfast before heading up.
10:30 AM
My additionLunch
Harmoni Foods · Honoka'a — Korean small plates
Tiny counter run by a former Four Seasons chef. Exclusively local sourcing — Big Island grass-fed beef, pasture-raised eggs, housemade gochujang and kimchi. Bibimbap bowls are the signature. A genuine hidden gem on the Hamakua Coast on the route to Saddle Road.
12:30 PM
My additionSnack
Tex Drive In · Honoka'a — fresh malasadas
Also in Honoka'a. Fresh Portuguese malasadas — classic or filled with chocolate, haupia, or guava. A Big Island institution. Car snack for the summit drive.
2:00 PM
Transit
Drive to Mauna Kea via Saddle Road (Hwy 200)
Full tank required before you start up — nearest gas is 35 miles from the VIS. Turn AC off on the ascent including defrost. ~1.5 hr from Waimea. Turn onto Mauna Kea Access Road at Mile Marker 28.
3:30 PM
Your pick (upgraded to summit)Summit 13,796 ft
Mauna Kea VIS → Summit — Jeep Wrangler 4LO
Your recommendation was VIS stargazing. The Wrangler now gives you the full summit road. Acclimate 30–60 min at VIS (9,200 ft). Rangers verify 4LO at the exit gate. Drive the unpaved gravel road to the summit — 40% less oxygen at the top, 33°F+ with windchill. Bring warm jackets, gloves, hats. Arrive before sunset for above-cloud views and the observatory domes opening. Summit road closes 30 min after sunset.
Use 4LO on the descent — do not ride your brakes. Brake failure from overheating is the most common incident. Engine braking in 4LO controls your speed on the way down.
7:30 PM
Transit
Descend to Kona via Saddle Road
~1.5 hr. The manta tour departs around 8:30–9 PM from Keauhou Bay, 7 miles south of Kona. Eat something light on the drive — you'll be in the water soon.
8:30 PM
My operator pick · your requestNight experience
Sea Paradise — Manta Ray Night Snorkel at Manta Village
You requested manta rays on night 3. Sea Paradise operates from Keauhou Bay at Manta Village — the original and most consistent site with 90%+ sighting rates on most nights. Float on a light board while mantas soar within inches feeding on plankton. Wingspans to 12 ft, no stingers. ~2 hrs, wetsuits provided. Free rebook guarantee if no mantas. ~$90/person. seaquesthawaii.com
Manta Village vs other sites. The three Kona manta sites — Manta Village near Keauhou is historically the most reliable. The other sites see mantas less consistently.

Day 04 · Thursday, March 26

Hawai'i Volcanoes
National Park

Rain-proof full day. Kilauea Iki crater hike, lava tube, Tuk Tuk Thai, volcano winery, and Kona Brewing for the post-volcano recovery dinner.

Removed due to rain: Captain Cook monument hike (steep clay, dangerous when wet) and Ke'ei cliff jump (single-lane lava mud road, impassable). Both are excellent in dry conditions — revisit if weather breaks.
6:30 AM
Work
Work block + park prep
Pack: rain jacket, closed-toe shoes, headlamp, 1 gallon water per person. Check nps.gov/havo for eruption status and trail conditions.
8:30 AM
Transit
Drive to Hawai'i Volcanoes National Park
~45 min from Hilo side or ~2 hrs from Kona. $30/vehicle or National Parks Annual Pass ($80).
9:30 AM
Hike
Kīlauea Iki Crater Trail — 4-mile loop
Best hike in the park. Descend through ʻōhiʻa rainforest to the solidified lava lake floor — steam vents still active, misty conditions are atmospheric. 360° crater views. Allow 2.5–3 hrs.
The crater floor was a lava lake in 1959. You're walking on 65 years of rock. Steam still rises from cracks beneath your feet.
12:30 PM
Explore
Thurston Lava Tube (Nāhuku)
Walk through a 500-year-old volcanic cave. Cool and otherworldly. ~20 min walk-through. If the main lot is full, park at Kīlauea Iki Overlook — trail connects.
1:30 PM
Your pickLunch
Tuk Tuk Thai — lunch near Volcano Village
Your recommendation for good Thai near the volcano. Outstanding food truck — avocado curry, spring rolls, pad thai, Thai iced tea. Wed–Sun 11 AM–6 PM only. Cash preferred.
3:00 PM
Scenic
Chain of Craters Road + Halemaʻumaʻu crater glow at dusk
Drive past ancient lava flows to where the road disappears into the ocean. Stay for the crater glow after dark if Kilauea is erupting. Check: nps.gov/havo. Bring a headlamp for the walk back.
5:00 PM
My additionTasting

Small winery near the park making guava, macadamia honey, and Symphony grape wines at 4,000 ft elevation. Quick tasting on the exit route — unique Big Island terroir nowhere else on earth.

Kona Brewing Co. — schooner & calamari
Your recommendation. Cold Longboard or Big Wave on tap, calamari, harbor views. Moved here from Day 1 as the post-volcano recovery dinner — you've earned it. Open until 9 PM.

Hilo

ʻAkaka Falls, Rainbow Falls, then an all-day Hilo food grazing tour — mochi, poke, Hilo Bay Café, Café Pesto, and the Hidden Nēnē speakeasy.

Designed for small plates throughout the day. All stops are covered from rain. The goal is to graze across many experiences rather than sit for one big meal.

Nature

Waterfall over a natural lava cave. Visit early — morning light produces rainbows in the mist. 1-min walk from parking. $5/person non-resident.

Most famous mochi shop on the island. Homemade mochi and manju in rotating seasonal flavors. Sells out by mid-morning almost every day. Call ahead: (808) 961-4766.
11:45 AM
Your pickSnack + shopping
KTA Super Store — poke counter + omiyage
Your recommendation. Fresh daily poke from the counter — multiple preparations. Stock up on omiyage: local chips, Kona coffee, Hawaiian snacks. Two Ladies mochi sometimes stocked here too: (808) 959-9111.
1:00 PM
My additionLunch
Hilo Bay Café — farm-to-table with bay views
Second-floor deck overlooking Hilo Bay and Mauna Kea. Hāmākua mushroom pot pie, ʻulu (breadfruit) potato gratin, fresh poke bowls. The Hāmākua mushrooms from local farms that thrive in rainy Hilo — you'll taste the difference. Strong wine list.
3:30 PM
My additionSmall plates
Café Pesto — Italian-Hawaiian wood-fired
Hilo institution since the 1980s. Wood-fired pizzas with Hawaiian ingredients — local fish, Hāmākua mushrooms, fresh herbs. Good grazing stop for a small plate between bigger experiences.
6:00 PM
My additionCocktails
The Hidden Nēnē — underground speakeasy
Underground bar on Furneaux Lane in downtown Hilo. Expertly crafted cocktails using local ingredients — pipikaula (dried beef), papaya, mango. Happy hour 6–8 PM. 4.7 stars, wildly popular with locals. Light bites available. A genuinely cool end to a great food day.
